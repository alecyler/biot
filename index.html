How to play + what you are seeing

Good first move: spawn a few flower types, let the world stabilize, then add hunters.

Builder: click segment chips or endpoints in the preview, change type, angle, and length, then spawn.

Inspector: click any biot in the world to inspect it and save survivors back into the builder.

Legend: green/photo feeds on light, red/predator bites, blue/propulsion moves, gold/reproduction lays eggs, gray/structure supports, silver/armor hardens.

Build ideas: structure + photo makes dependable flowers; perception + brain + propulsion makes rovers; launcher/lightning/flame/frost shine once food is stable.

Hidden-feeling tricks: glow boosts light harvesting in dim spots but also increases visibility; camo does the reverse. Advanced segments wake up when a biot reaches maturity.

Field note:
Field Guide Tips, legend, and build nudges

Start with flowers, let food accumulate, then introduce hunters. Use the builder to save stable species and reseed them later.

Quick legend
  • Bright zones feed plant-heavy biots.
  • Blue gravity pulls; red gravity pushes.
  • Fire zones hurt and heat the tank.
  • Green pellets are carrion or spores.
  • Pale rings usually mark young or selected biots.
Build suggestions
  • Flower core: photo + reproduction + structure.
  • Mobile grazer: photo + glide or propulsion + reproduction.
  • Smart hunter: predator + propulsion + perception + brain.
  • Late-game weapons like launcher, flame, frost, and lightning work best after a food web exists.
  • Glow boosts light harvesting in dim spots, but it also increases visibility. Camo does the opposite.
Hidden-ish unlock notes
  • Eyes and brains are most useful when paired with movement. A stationary thinker just burns energy.
  • Some advanced segments only become active after maturity, so juveniles may look simpler than the adults they become.
  • Saved favorites can come back later through cuckoo eggs, so stable species are worth preserving.
  • Portrait is best for active play. Landscape is better when you mostly want to watch the tank.
